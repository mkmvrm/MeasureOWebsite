About Measure O

The Isla Vista Recreation and Park District (IVRPD) has been maintaining the parks in Isla Vista since 1972. Their mission is, and always has been, to improve and protect the quality of life through park stewardship, community engagement, and recreation and cultural programming.

Current IVRPD funding allows for moderate levels of park maintenance and a limited amount of recreational programming for children. They also secure grants to operate trash removal and graffiti abatement programs for the entire community. Frequent community events and festivals in the parks are made possible by their enthusiastic partnerships with student groups, UCSB staff and faculty, and other community organizations.

IVRPD outreach and student and community sponsored engagement clearly show that the 52 acres of parkland maintained by IVRPD are essential to the quality of life in this otherwise cramped community. The same feedback also identifies the desire and need for the park district to provide additional facilities and programming.
